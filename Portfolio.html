My Story

I grew up in the Philippines, and during middle school, I was known as a creative, smart, and cooperative student. I had many friends and was well-connected through my family's social circle.

When I was 8 years old, my mom moved to America and later remarried, starting a new chapter with a blended family. Fast forward to high school, I consistently ranked at the top of my class and often led group projects, earning a reputation as someone reliable, quick-witted, calm under pressure, and capable of leadership. Eventually, my mom called to let me know that I, along with my siblings, would be moving to America to live with her.

After arriving, I enrolled at Waterloo High School, where I continued to stay cooperative, focused, and eager to learn. I made it a priority to adapt to the new environment and culture, paying attention to the small details from communication styles to social behavior so I could adjust, connect, and grow.

After graduating from Waterloo High School with a 3.5 GPA, I was preparing to join the U.S. Air Force. It had been a goal I held onto for a long time, but just before enlistment, my medical records revealed that I had an allergic reaction to shellfish. Even though I had no trouble eating it, I was still required to carry an EpiPen, and that small detail was enough to close the door on my opportunity to serve.

At first, it felt like the path I had worked hard for had suddenly disappeared. But instead of letting that moment define me, I decided to move forward.

I enrolled at Finger Lakes Community College, determined to make the most of this new chapter. What began as a disappointment turned into a blessing, I discovered new skills, gained knowledge I never expected, and learned how to adapt in the face of setbacks. In the process, I grew stronger, more disciplined, and more confident in carving my own path toward the future.
